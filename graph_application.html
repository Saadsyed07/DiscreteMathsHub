Applications of Graph Theory

Graph Theory plays a vital role in solving real-world problems by representing objects and their relationships through vertices and edges. It forms the foundation of numerous applications in Computer Science and other fields such as biology, geography, and social networks.

üíª Applications in Computer Science

Computer Networks

Graphs model the network topology: routers, switches, hosts as vertices and links as edges.

  • Shortest path algorithms: Dijkstra, Bellman-Ford.
  • Routing, connectivity and resilience analysis.

Social Networks

Users are nodes; friendships/follows are edges.

  • Recommendations using graph traversal.
  • Community detection & influencer ranking.

Database & Web Structure

Graphs model entity relationships and link structure of the web.

  • PageRank-style ranking algorithms.
  • Graph databases (Neo4j) for relationship queries.

Compiler Design

Graphs represent syntax/ASTs, control-flow, and dependencies.

  • Register allocation via graph coloring.
  • Dependency ordering and optimization passes.

AI / ML

Graphs power knowledge representation and Graph Neural Networks (GNNs).

  • GNNs for learning on relational data.
  • Path planning in robotics with BFS/DFS/A*.

üåç Applications in Other Fields

Biology

Graph models of protein interactions, gene networks and neural connections.

  • Study evolutionary trees and pathways.

Transportation & Logistics

Roads, railways and flight routes modeled as graphs to optimize routing.

  • GPS & mapping systems rely on graph algorithms.

Chemistry

Molecules as graphs (atoms = vertices, bonds = edges) for similarity and reaction prediction.

Electrical Engineering

Circuits as graphs for connectivity and flow analysis.

Sociology

Analyze social structures and influence patterns using graph analytics.
