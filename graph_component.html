Connected Components in Graphs

In Graph Theory, a Connected Component is a subgraph in which any two vertices are connected to each other by paths, and no vertex in the subgraph is connected to any vertex outside it.

  • Connected Graph: Every vertex is reachable from every other vertex within the same graph.
  • Disconnected Graph: There exist two or more subsets (components) of vertices such that no vertex in one subset has an edge to any vertex in another subset.
  • Connected Component: Each isolated group of connected nodes is known as a connected component.

Connected components help us understand the structure and relationships within a graph:

  • In social networks, each component represents a separate group of connected people.
  • In computer networks, they show which computers can communicate with each other.
  • In image processing, components represent connected pixels of the same color.
